Brief Introduction to Autism

Autism is a highly variable neurodevelopmental disorder whose symptoms first appear during infancy or childhood, and generally follows a steady course without remission. Autistic people may be severely impaired in some respects but average, or even superior, in others. Overt symptoms gradually begin after the age of six months, become established by age two or three years and tend to continue through adulthood, although often in more muted form.

Source: Wikipedia

Symptoms of Autism

It is distinguished by a characteristic triad of symptoms: impairments in social interaction, impairments in communication, and repetitive behavior.

  • Does not respond to name by 9 months of age
  • Does not show facial expressions like happy, sad, angry, and surprised by 9 months of age
  • Does not play simple interactive games
  • Does not share interests with others by 15 months of age (e.g. shows you an object that they like)
  • Has obsessive interests or Must follow certain routines
  • Lines up toys or other objects and gets upset when order is changed
  • Flaps hands, rocks body, or spins self in circles
Source: Center for Disease Contral and Prevention
